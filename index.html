Tetris Game Overview

Tetris is a tile-matching puzzle video game originally designed and programmed by Russian game designer Alexey Pajitnov. It was released on June 6, 1984, while Pajitnov was working for the Dorodnicyn Computing Centre of the Academy of Science of the USSR in Moscow. In Tetris, players manipulate tetrominoesâ€”shapes made up of four square blocks that can be rotated and moved horizontally as they descend from the top of the screen. The goal of the game is to arrange the tetrominoes so that they form a solid horizontal line without any gaps.

In Tetris, you have to move and rotate different shaped blocks called "tetrominoes" as they fall from the top of the screen. Your goal is to fit the tetrominoes together to create horizontal lines without any gaps. When you complete a line, it disappears, and you earn points. As you progress through the game, the tetrominoes will fall faster and faster, making it more challenging to fit them together. The game ends when the stack of tetrominoes reaches the top of the screen.

  • The name "Tetris" comes from a combination of the Greek word "tetra" (meaning "four") and Pajitnov's favorite sport, tennis.
  • Tetris has been played on a variety of platforms, including arcade machines, home video game consoles, computers, and even calculators.
  • The game's popularity skyrocketed in 1989 when it was bundled with Nintendo's Game Boy handheld console. Tetris went on to become one of the most popular games on the platform, and it is still widely associated with the original Game Boy.
  • Tetris has been studied by researchers for its effects on the brain. One study found that playing Tetris can reduce the occurrence of intrusive memories, a symptom of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
  • In 2020, the Tetris world record for the fastest time to clear 40 lines was set by Koryan, a Japanese player who cleared the lines in just 16.85 seconds.
